# Phonics to Physics — Membership Launch Plan

*A practical plan to launch a subscription resource membership without trying to build everything at once. Start with one niche, a small starter library, and a weekly live session — then grow.*

---

## Part 1 — Which niche to launch first

You can't launch Year 1–A-level in every subject at once, and you shouldn't. Pick the single niche with the best mix of **market size, willingness to pay, year-round demand, and reusable content you already have.** Here's the comparison:

| Niche | Market size | Willingness to pay | Demand pattern | Content reuse | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **GCSE Science & Maths** | Very large — nearly every teen | High (exam stakes) | Year-round, peaks Jan–June | Excellent (past papers, topics) | ⭐ **Launch here** |
| 11+ prep | Medium, affluent | Very high (anxious parents) | Seasonal (peaks Y5) | Good (practice papers) | Strong phase 2 |
| KS2 / Year 6 SATs | Large | Medium | Seasonal (spring) | Good | Later |
| A-level Maths/Physics | Smaller | High per head | Year-round | Good but bespoke | Niche add-on |
| Primary reading / phonics | Broad | Lower for a membership | Year-round | Good | Better as 1:1 / parent guides |

### Recommendation: **launch with GCSE Science & Maths.**

Why it wins:
- **Biggest addressable market** — almost every secondary student sits these, so you're not fishing in a small pond.
- **Exam pressure = willingness to pay.** Parents and students will pay monthly for structured help toward a grade.
- **Year-round demand** that intensifies from January to June — not a two-month seasonal spike.
- **Your content is the most reusable here** — past papers, mark schemes and topic explainers you already use in 1:1 sessions translate straight into library material.

**11+ is your strong phase-2 expansion** (very high willingness to pay), and A-level Maths/Physics a natural premium add-on once the core is running.

### Suggested pricing
Keep it simple to start: **one tier, ~£15–19/month**, "GCSE Science & Maths membership." Founding members get a discounted rate for life (great launch incentive). Add phase-specific tiers later. The **weekly live session** is what justifies a recurring price — without it, people download everything and cancel.

## Part 2 — 4-week starter-library content plan

**Assumptions:** roughly 1–2 hours on a few evenings plus some weekend time. The goal is **not** a complete library — it's a credible *starter* set plus a live session, enough to open to founding members at the end of week 4. You then keep adding ~2–3 pieces a week forever.

**Target starter library (end of week 4):**
~20 worksheets · 4 past-paper walkthroughs · 6 short topic videos · 3 revision guides · 1 parent guide · a weekly live Q&A running.

### Week 1 — Structure & first content
- Choose your exam boards (e.g. AQA) and write a simple **topic map** for GCSE Maths and Combined Science so the library has a skeleton.
- Create **6–8 worksheets** on the *highest-value, most-searched topics* (e.g. algebra rearranging, ratio, forces, energy, cell biology). Reuse what you already teach.
- Make **1 knowledge organiser** (one-page topic summary) as a template you'll repeat.
- **Run your first weekly live Q&A** (even to a tiny group) — get the habit going.

### Week 2 — Past papers (your highest-value asset)
- Produce **3–4 past-paper walkthroughs**: screen-record yourself working through questions with commentary, plus annotated mark schemes. These are what students value most.
- Add **6 more worksheets** filling gaps in the topic map.
- Live Q&A #2.

### Week 3 — Video explainers & checking understanding
- Record **5–6 short topic explainer videos** (5–10 min each) on the topics students find hardest.
- Add **3–4 quizzes** (self-marking if the platform allows) so members can test themselves.
- Add the remaining worksheets to reach ~20.
- Live Q&A #3.

### Week 4 — Polish, onboarding & launch
- Write **2 revision guides / checklists** ("How to revise GCSE Science," an exam-technique guide).
- Write **1 parent guide** ("How to support your teen through GCSEs").
- Build a simple **"Start here" path** so new members aren't overwhelmed.
- **Open to founding members** at your discounted launch rate. Announce to current 1:1 families and their networks first.
- Live Q&A #4 — now a fixture.

### After launch — the cadence that keeps people paying
Add **2–3 new pieces per week** (a worksheet, a walkthrough, a video) and run the **weekly live session**. That steady drip + live access is the whole reason a membership retains, and it compounds into a serious library over a year with only a few hours a week.
